Quantum Jumping and Parallel Universes

In 2009, paranormal consultant Fiona Broome coined the term Mandela Effect (ME), describing the conflict between people’s memories of the past and their current reality. The Mandela Effect refers to a phenomenon in which large groups of people share the same or similar memories that do not align with documented history.

Prior to Fiona Broome, researcher Starfire Tor introduced the term “Time Shift Effect”, along with what she described as the “Time Shift Living Dead Phenomena”. These ideas have been linked by some to what was believed to have occurred with Nelson Mandela, forming part of the foundation for today’s Mandela Effect discussions. She also explored concepts such as timelines, the “Core Matrix”, time travel, co-existing timelines, Dark Matter Aberration—described as a potential source of imbalance—and the Unified Field Theory of Psi. Starfire Tor considered these ideas to represent a kind of Holy Grail of hidden and forbidden knowledge.

Thousands of people report disturbances in memory across many areas of life. Differences are recalled in animals, art, cartoons, games, and geographical or landscape features. Some even suggest changes within our constellations. Variations in memories of historical events, landmarks, logos, films, quotes, music, and the spelling of names—of people, products, and companies—have also been noted. There are even suggestions that aspects of human anatomy may have subtly shifted.

A range of explanations has been proposed. A small number believe humanity is experiencing a form of dark or negative influence, potentially leading towards large-scale conflict. Others hold a more spiritual perspective—that humanity is shifting energetically towards a higher state of awareness, aligning with the “new Earth” described in Revelation 21:1–4: “Then I saw a new heaven and a new earth… He will wipe every tear from their eyes.” In this view, suffering is gradually coming to an end, and a future of peace and harmony becomes possible as individuals awaken from lower states of thought and living.

Regardless of the theories surrounding this phenomenon, it may serve us well to develop a calm acceptance of our present reality. Each individual holds a unique perception of the world, shaped by personal experience and the interpretation of both physical and historical evidence.

I extend my thanks to the many people, web pages, and sources whose research and contributions have supported the ongoing discussion of the Mandela Effect.

Within these discussions, a quiet awakening often occurs—as knowledge expands and the mind opens to alternative possibilities. I invite you to explore and reflect upon this compilation of the Mandela Effect experience.
